So I DIY'ed a NAS on a shoestring budget...

...and tl;dr if you have a few hundred lying around, you can probably just buy something synology makes and call it a day.

If instead of cash you happen to a have very large stack of media storage devices, chances are you have two with similar storage capacities and that means you too can jank together a technically functional NAS!

You'll need:

  • a raspberry pi. I recommend at least a 3. My Zero had some issues with the power draw.
  • 2x hard drives with similar capacity. The Pi 3 has 4 USB 3.0

You'll probably need:

  • a power brick
  • 2x y-split power USB cord

You probably won't need because I bought them and debugged stuff:

  • USB voltometer
